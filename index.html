Forum + Work Sans

An elegant, yet quirky combination

Forum is a serif font that’s elegant, but with a few unique characters that give it a quirky flair. Work Sans shares some similar characteristics while providing good contrast, making these two the perfect pair. They lean feminine and work well for businesses in creative industries, or coaches who work with creatives.

These fonts pair well to create a sophisticated, yet modern vibe. They have great legibility but are still playful, making them a nice duo.

Six Caps + Open Sans

Great for neutral audiences

Have a lot to say, but not a lot of space to say it? Six Caps is perfect for tight spaces and serious titles. Open Sans makes an ideal body font pairing because of its neutral yet friendly appearance. Use this corporate pair for agencies and consultants with neutral or masculine-leaning audiences.

This combination is bold and eyecatching, yet still has a commercial feel. It is modern and can be applied to a variety of different projects.

Rozha One + Questrial

Great for headlines and body copy

Rozha One’s high-contrast forms create an extreme difference between its letters’ thick and thin strokes, which make it an excellent choice for large headlines. Questrial has a modern style that makes for a great body copy pairing, as it’s full-circle curves blend with other fonts while still maintaining its uniqueness.

This pairing works great for a creative project that will want to catch the attention of any viewer. This pairing has nice juxtaposition and feels timeless yet original.
